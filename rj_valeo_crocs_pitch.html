03 — When things break

Pattern recognition
from real incidents.

Three incident types. One response model. I've been in all three of these rooms.

Feed failure

Rithum stops syncing product data to Amazon. Listings go dark. Inventory counters freeze. Orders stop. Revenue impact is immediate and visible.

High severity Revenue impact Integration
Lived this: Amazon FBA onboarding for a boutique publisher. Feed formatting failures, listing suppression, inventory sync lag causing oversells. Real incidents, real consequences, real fixes.

PM response pattern

01
Identify the break in the pipeline — SAP → OIC → Rithum. Which node owns the failure.
02
Route to the right team immediately. Integration, not marketplace ops, owns the pipe.
03
Set the SLA clock. Communicate business impact to stakeholders before they ask.
04
Track to resolution. Document the failure mode and the fix for the next incident.

Checkout defect

SFCC cart or payment flow breaks after a release. Customers can't complete purchases. Revenue stops on the DTC site. Clock is running from the moment it's detected.

Critical Post-release SFCC
Lived this: led post-launch stabilization windows at Understood.org and A&E across 12–15 concurrent release streams. Defect triage bridges, go/no-go decisions, hotfix coordination under pressure.

PM response pattern

01
Convene defect triage bridge immediately — Engineering, QA, Product in the room.
02
Assess severity and scope. Isolated cart bug or systemic checkout failure?
03
Hotfix vs. rollback decision — made with Engineering lead and Product. PM owns the call and communicates it.
04
ETA to stakeholders. Post-launch monitoring window extended. Incident documented.

Listing misconfiguration

Products appear in the wrong regional marketplace — wrong language, wrong pricing tier, wrong fulfillment model. Customers think they're buying something they're not. Suppression risk is immediate.

Medium severity Rithum / Regional Feed mapping
Lived this: Amazon FBA onboarding for a boutique publisher. Books listed in French and Spanish marketplaces without translated content. Customers bought and returned. I triaged the feed mapping error, coordinated the delist, and rebuilt the config.

PM response pattern

01
Isolate the feed mapping error. Which SKUs, which markets, which field is wrong.
02
Coordinate with ops and content teams. Delist affected SKUs while the fix is staged.
03
Confirm suppression risk with marketplace team. Some errors trigger automated listing removal — get ahead of it.
04
Resubmit with verification. QA the relisted products before marking resolved.
